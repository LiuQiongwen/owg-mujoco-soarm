## SMART Research Goal

**Topic**: Pairwise Grasp Scoring with BPR for Open-World Robotic Grasping

---

### Novel Angle

**Gap**: Existing grasp quality estimators (GraspNet-1B, ContactGraspNet, GIGA) rely on *pointwise regression* or *classification* against labeled grasp quality scores. This forces models to learn absolute quality thresholds that poorly generalize across novel object geometries and task semantics. Pairwise ranking (BPR-style) sidesteps the need for absolute quality labels — yet **no published work applies pairwise learning-to-rank to language-conditioned grasp selection in open-world settings**.

**Why timely (2025-2026)**:
- VLM-grounded grasping (RT-2, OpenVLA, SpatialVLM) is now mainstream, but grasp *selection* post-VLM is still an open problem.
- BPR-style objectives gained traction in embodied preference learning (RLHF for robotics, 2024) but haven't crossed into geometric grasp ranking.
- The pairwise formulation naturally aligns with human/LLM preference data collection — which is scalable without physical robot execution.

**Differentiation from prior work**:
- Not a benchmark comparison of existing scorers.
- Not a new grasp detector — we *rank* candidates generated by an existing planner.
- Novel: **language-conditioned BPR with geometry features** trained on synthetic pairwise preference data, evaluated on real/simulated open-world objects.

---

### Scope

Single paper focused on: *Can pairwise BPR training with language+geometry features outperform pointwise grasp scorers for novel objects in open-world grasping?*

Ablations:
1. BPR vs. pointwise MSE loss
2. Language conditioning on/off
3. Geometry feature types (surface normals vs. curvature vs. raw depth)

---

### SMART Goal

| Dimension | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| **Specific** | Train LGGSN with BPR pairwise loss on synthetic grasp pair preferences; evaluate on YCB + novel household objects in PyBullet |
| **Measurable** | Grasp success rate (GSR) ≥ 60% on 10 YCB objects, ≥ 5 pp improvement over random baseline |
| **Achievable** | Single GPU (RTX 3090/4090), training < 4 hours on synthetic pairs, eval via PyBullet simulation |
| **Relevant** | Directly advances open-world grasping under language grounding — core OWG project goal |
| **Time-bound** | Reproducible result within 2 weeks; paper-ready within 6 weeks |

---

### Constraints

- **Compute**: Single GPU, no cloud cluster; training budget < 4 GPU-hours per run
- **Data**: Synthetic pairwise preferences from PyBullet simulation (no real robot required)
- **Tools**: PyBullet, Open3D, existing LGGSN backbone, VLM grounding (existing pipeline)
- **Baselines**: Random grasp selection, pointwise LGGSN, GraspNet-1B quality score re-ranking

---

### Success Criteria

1. **Primary**: BPR-trained LGGSN achieves ≥ 55% GSR on YCB held-out objects (vs. ~45% random baseline observed in current experiments)
2. **Secondary**: Pairwise model generalizes to 3+ completely unseen object categories without fine-tuning
3. **Ablation signal**: Language conditioning provides ≥ 3 pp lift over geometry-only pairwise ranking
4. **Publishable bar**: Results competitive with or exceeding GraspNet-1B re-ranking on open-world split; clear ablation story
