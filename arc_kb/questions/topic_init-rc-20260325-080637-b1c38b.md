## SMART Research Goal: Pairwise Grasp Scoring for Open-World Grasping

---

**Topic**: Pairwise grasp quality ranking via learned scoring in open-world robotic manipulation

---

**Novel Angle**

Most grasp quality estimators (GraspNet-1Billion, Contact-GraspNet, AnyGrasp) assign *absolute* quality scores to individual grasps. The **pairwise comparative ranking** (BPR) framing — "is grasp A better than grasp B?" — is well-motivated by preference learning but has not been systematically studied for *open-world*, zero-shot generalization to novel object categories.

What makes this timely (2024–2026):
1. **Foundation model grounding** (CLIP, DINO, SAM2) now gives geometry-agnostic object representations, enabling pairwise comparisons that generalize without per-object training.
2. **Preference learning renaissance**: RLHF/DPO showed that pairwise data is easier to label and generalizes better than absolute ratings — this insight has barely reached grasp scoring.
3. **Gap**: No published work evaluates whether BPR grasp ranking generalizes under *semantic distribution shift* (seen object categories → unseen), whereas absolute-score models are known to degrade on novel materials/shapes.

This differs from standard approaches by: (a) framing grasp selection as a ranking problem rather than regression, (b) conditioning pairwise features on VLM-grounded semantic context, and (c) explicitly evaluating *cross-category zero-shot transfer*.

---

**Scope**

Single paper: Does BPR-based pairwise grasp scoring outperform absolute-score baselines when **tested on object categories not seen during training**, using a VLM-grounded feature extractor as the shared backbone?

---

**SMART Goal**

> Train a pairwise grasp scorer (LGGSN-BPR) conditioned on CLIP/DINO semantic features, evaluate on YCB + a held-out novel-category split, and demonstrate ≥10 pp improvement in grasp success rate over the random-selection and absolute-score baselines, within 6 weeks on a single GPU.

- **Specific**: Pairwise BPR scorer with semantic conditioning, evaluated on novel categories
- **Measurable**: Grasp success rate (%) in PyBullet, reported over ≥50 trials per condition
- **Achievable**: LGGSN pairwise infrastructure already exists in this repo; YCB dataset available
- **Relevant**: Directly addresses the current project's Stage 4 accuracy bottleneck
- **Time-bound**: 6 weeks (by ~2026-05-06)

- No real-robot access; simulation-only evaluation
